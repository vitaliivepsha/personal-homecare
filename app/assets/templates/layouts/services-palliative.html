Palliative, Alzheimer's, Dementia, & Hospice Support

back to Services

We work to help your loved one continue to function independently with support, keeping them safe, helping them avoid stress, focusing on their strengths, and keeping the lines of communication open so we can understand what your loved one needs at all times and ensure they feel comfortable receiving help.

  • Encouraging Mental Activity — We help your loved one keep appointments, plan things, remember words and names, and engage in positive mental activity through things like conversation, interactions and games.

  • Minimizing Behavioral Symptoms — Person-centered care and communication (getting to know and understand your loved one and their preferences), sensory stimulation (like a hand massage) and listening to music are three evidence-based therapies we use to help reduce agitation and other challenging behaviors for people with dementia. Our caregivers actively work to read your loved one’s body language, create comfortable routines, and reduce stress in their daily life.

  • Enhancing Safety & Comfort — Your loved one’s safety and comfort is our top priority.

  • Respite for Family Caregivers — When you’re caring for a loved one, it’s also essential that you get rest so that you can continue to bring your best to your loved one. We can help ensure your loved one is well-cared-for while you take a much-deserved break.

  • Emotional Support to the Family — At A+ Home Care, we understand that this is a difficult time for our clients and their families! We aren’t just invested in providing excellent care to your loved one; we also want to be there for you with a positive attitude and the emotional support that will help you keep moving forward.

  • Supplemental Care Services — Whatever your loved one needs, we can help.
